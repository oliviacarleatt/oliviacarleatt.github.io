As far as class assignments go, I decided to revisit my soundscape and improve the overall organization and feel of the sound clips. My group chose the theme of “inside a beer bottle.” For my first submission, it was a bit messy, choppy, and rough, and my overall plan didn’t get executed properly. This time, I started over, but I used the same sound clips as the first time. I worked on the organization of the clips to tell a story. I outlined the order of the sounds to mimic someone drinking a beer and what it would be like to be trapped inside of the bottle, also reflecting the drunken state of the person drinking the beer. The audio piece begins with friends celebrating over a winning sports game. The clinking and opening of the glass leads to gulping and an ambient floating sound that’s actually supposed to be under the water in a pool. I pretended there was a tiny shrunken human submerged in the beer holding their breath. As the soundscape goes on, the drinker goes to the bathroom and as they come out to get another drink the drunken state catches up with them. The background voices slow down and the gulping gets a lower pitch. The person in the bottle slows down with the drinker and the scene is supposed to sound like slow motion. It ends with shattering glass. This could be interpreted as the beginning of a fight or simply the drunken person dropping the bottle. I think this piece was much cleaner, concise, and effective. For the first audio submission, the narrative wasn’t as clear and was quite unpleasant to listen to. This time around I focused less on playing with echoes and strange sound effects and focused more on combining the clips to portray a narrative, and manipulating the sound slightly to mimic slow motion.
